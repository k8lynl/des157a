10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills

October 24, 2024

Link to article

The article “10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills” discusses methods to encourage deeper visual thinking through images. The most striking images are ones that foster communication and collaboration with others, and ultimately the sharing of new ideas, questions, and knowledge. In this article, I noticed that many of the photos that it featured were particularly striking because they had some sort of story behind them. The photos all had a living creature or human in them, or were intriguing because they were taken in a foreign place. The photos that featured some sort of movement were especially interesting as well.

One website that uses both images and interaction that I found was cosmos.so. This website is a web application that is similar to Pinterest and allows users to gather and collect images that they find across the web. By using scrolling effects, parallax, animations, and simply creating collages of images on their website, the user is visually intrigued all while learning more about what cosmos has to offer. My main takeaway from observing this website is that images and interactions are crucial to keeping a user engaged when you are trying to tell a visual story.

Overuse of Overlays: How to Avoid Misusing Lightboxes

October 22, 2024

Link to article

The article “Overuse of Overlays: How to Avoid Misusing Lightboxes” discusses the mass popularity of overlays among designers, and the situations in which overlays should and should not be used. Overlays should be used in moderation, and are intended for users to view content, complete a form, or make a decision. If the purpose is to view content, the content should be brief as well. Overlays should not be lengthy to the point where the user must scroll and use multiple navigation mechanisms within it. When completing a form or making a decision, the actions, as well as the cancel button should be clearly visible and explicit.

Overlays often pose an obstacle to users as they interrupt the user from their current action. To avoid this sense of interruption, overlays should be used in the beginning of the user flow. An alternative is to make the overlay open by the user’s own action, such as clicking on a button to open the overlay.

The main takeaway from this article, however, is that overlays should only be used if there is a compelling case for why the overlay content should not be presented on the original page. These cases include actions with serious consequences, required information before proceeding to the next step, or content that is urgent.

Best practices for form design

October 14, 2024

Link to article

The article “Best practices for form design” by Salim Ansari discusses the various ways a designer can improve the user experience of forms. Many of the methods to improve form UX follows the simple guideline of “less is more”. This principle ultimately minimizes the user’s “cognitive load and visual noise,” preventing the user from being overwhelmed by too many steps or information. Another guideline is to make things as clear and labelled as possible at all times. This prevents any confusion on the user’s side, and assists them in completing the form faster with more clarity. From colors to placement of text to the phrasing of instructions and feedback, even the simplistic of design choices are crucial to crafting an easy experience for form users.

An example of a website form with excellent UX is Airtable.
